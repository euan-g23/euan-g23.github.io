Finite Element Analysis of a Rotor Hub

2025 · MSc Project

A finite element analysis of a helicopter rotor hub to investigate structural behaviour under loading. The project explores mesh sensitivity, stress distribution, and a simple design refinement. A secondary study introduces topology optimisation to examine stiffness-to-weight trade-offs.

At a Glance

  • Methods: FEA, mesh refinement, optimisation
  • Tools: Abaqus
  • Focus: Structural response, modelling choices, performance trade-offs

Problem Context

Rotor hubs transfer loads from rotor blades to the central shaft and are subject to significant mechanical stress. This study examines how modelling choices influence predicted stress distribution and overall structural behaviour.

Approach

A simplified geometry was analysed in Abaqus with representative loading and boundary conditions. Multiple mesh configurations were tested to assess solution stability, and different element types were compared to understand their influence on results.

Key Results

Mesh refinement influenced both stress and displacement predictions, with some configurations showing more consistent behaviour than others. Areas of high stress were observed near geometric features associated with load transfer.

Design Insight

A simple geometric modification improved load distribution across the structure, reducing localised stress concentrations and highlighting the importance of structural continuity.

Optimisation Study

A topology optimisation study explored how reducing material volume affects stiffness. Results showed that moderate material reduction can maintain structural performance while improving efficiency.

Key Takeaways

The project highlights how modelling decisions—such as mesh selection and simplification—can influence simulation outcomes. It also demonstrates the balance required between model detail, computational cost, and practical engineering insight.
