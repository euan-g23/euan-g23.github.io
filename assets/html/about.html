Euan

I am a graduate aerospace engineer with a BEng (Hons) and MSc. My background is strongest in numerical modelling and simulation, but I am broadly interested in how engineering decisions are made, tested, and justified across aerospace projects.

A lot of my academic work involved tackling complex problems where assumptions, trade-offs, and time constraints mattered just as much as technical detail. I enjoy understanding how different design choices affect performance and feasibility, and I am comfortable questioning results, refining approaches, and explaining conclusions clearly.

This site documents selected BEng and MSc projects as short case studies. Each project outlines the problem being tackled, the approach taken, the key decisions made along the way, and the limitations identified. The aim is to show how academic engineering work can translate into practical, real-world thinking.
