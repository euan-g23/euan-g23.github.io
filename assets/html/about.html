Euan

I am a graduate aerospace engineer with a BEng (Hons) and MSc, with a primary interest in numerical modelling, CFD, and simulation-driven analysis. My academic work has focused on transient, high-fidelity problems where modelling assumptions and solver configuration significantly influence results.

I am particularly interested in how modelling choices translate into engineering insight. Rather than treating simulations as black-box tools, I focus on understanding assumptions, numerical stability, sensitivity to parameters, and the limits of model validity. I place emphasis on explaining results clearly and acknowledging uncertainty where appropriate.

This site documents selected BEng and MSc projects as concise case studies. Each project focuses on the problem context, technical approach, key decisions, and limitations, with the aim of demonstrating how academic simulation work can be translated into industry-relevant engineering thinking.
