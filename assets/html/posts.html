MSc Thesis:

High-speed water droplet impact was modelled using a compressible, multiphase CFD approach to assess the ability of numerical simulations to capture transient pressure response and shock formation. A structured mesh sensitivity study highlighted where agreement with reference studies is strong, and where numerical and temporal resolution limit the capture of fine-scale dynamics. The project demonstrates both the applicability and practical limitations of CFD for erosion-relevant impact problems.

Dynamic Aeroelastic Flutter Modelling (MSc Coursework)

Reduced-order aeroelastic modelling of a two-dimensional aerofoil using MATLAB and Simulink, extended from a 2DOF system to include a trailing-edge flap. The project investigates flutter onset, dynamic stability, and the limitations encountered when increasing model fidelity.
